Candidate A - Alex Rodriguez

Background:
Grew up in Metroville, where life was tough and money was scarce.

Lost his parents when he was young and had to work hard to survive, doing odd jobs to make ends meet.

Discovered for his talent and became an actor and performer.

Seeing how tough life was for others in Metroville, Juan decided to get into politics to help make things better.

With his success as Mayor, Juan decided to aim even higher and run for a job in the national government.

Some people doubted whether Juan could do the job because he came from entertainment, not politics.

Juan has always fought for the poor and those who need help the most, because he knows what it's like to struggle.

Candidate B - Diego Martinez

Background:
Son of a former powerful leader, General Juan Martinez and Maria Martinez.

Entered politics in the early 2000s, starting as Mayor of Santa Rosa and later becoming Governor of Mariposa province.

In 2016, Diego ran for Vice President but lost amidst allegations of unfair voting.

Diego disputed the election results, leading to protests and legal battles that lasted for years.

Faced criticism due to his family's past authoritarian rule, with allegations of human rights abuses and corruption lingering.

His candidacy sparked debates about his family's legacy and the future direction of San Cristobal, shaping political discussions in the country.

Candidate C - Lara Vanguardia

Background:
Grew up in a small town in the Philippines, where she witnessed firsthand the struggles of ordinary people in her community.

Studied law and became a dedicated lawyer, known for her passion for social justice and helping those in need, especially marginalized groups.

Hesitant about politics, Maria's perspective changed when her husband, Juan, a beloved community leader, passed away unexpectedly. Encouraged by her husband's supporters, Lara decides to carry on his legacy by running for Mayor of their city, San Isidro.

Lara's leadership and dedication catch the attention of the national stage, leading her to run for Vice President of the Philippines in 2025. Her campaign centers on continuing her work at the local level, advocating for the rights and welfare of ordinary Filipinos.

Lara faces skepticism from political opponents who question her lack of experience in national politics. However, she remains undeterred, determined to prove her critics wrong through hard work and dedication.

Candidate D - Juanito Garcia

Background:
Grew up poor.

Had to help his family by doing odd jobs and selling things.

Discovered he was good at boxing and started training hard.

Became famous for winning boxing matches and earned the nickname "Juan the Warrior."

Helped his community by giving money to schools and hospitals and helping kids who didn't have much.

Decided to become a politician to help even more people. He ran for a position in the city government.

Juanito wanted to make sure kids could go to school, everyone had access to healthcare, and young people had opportunities to succeed.

Some people didn't think Juanito could be a good politician because he was a boxer. But he kept working hard to prove them wrong.
