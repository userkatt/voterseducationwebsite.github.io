An individual must confirm their identity when submitting an application, and acceptable identification documents include the following:

1 / 13
Phil ID
2 / 13
Employee's ID with the signature of the employer or authorized representative
3 / 13
Postal ID
4 / 13
PWD Discount ID
5 / 13
Student's ID or library card, signed by the school authority
6 / 13
Senior Citizen's ID
7 / 13
Driver's license
8 / 13
NBI clearance
9 / 13
Passport
10 / 13
SSS/GSIS ID
11 / 13
Integrated Bar of the Philippine (IBP) ID
12 / 13
License issued by the Professional Regulatory Commission (PRC)
13 / 13
Certificate of Confirmation issued by the National Commission on Indigenous Peoples (NCIP) in case of members of ICCs or IPs (OR ANY VALID ID)

In the event that the individual lacks any of the aforementioned identification documents, the applicant has the option to be positively identified through a sworn oath administered by any registered voter within the precinct where registration is intended or by a relative within the fourth civil degree of consanguinity or affinity. It is important to note that no registered voter or relative is permitted to vouch for more than three (3) applicants.

It should be emphasized that Community Tax Certificates (cedula) and PNP clearance will not be recognized as valid identification documents for the purpose of registration.

In the event that the applicant is unable to establish their identity through the aforementioned methods or documents, they will be ineligible to receive an application form, and any pre-accomplished application form submitted will not be accepted.
